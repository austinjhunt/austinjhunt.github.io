Snap n' Sell Privacy Policy

A plain-English summary of what data Snap n' Sell collects, what it does with it, and the controls you have over it.

Last updated: June 17, 2026

The short version

Snap n' Sell helps you generate marketplace listings from a photo. To do that, the app needs to:

  • Know who you are (sign-in), so your listings are tied to your account.
  • Receive a photo from your camera or photo library so we can analyze it with AI and generate a listing.
  • Track how many listings you've generated today, so we can enforce a small daily limit.

We don't sell your data, we don't show ads, and we don't track you across other apps. You can delete your account and all associated data from inside the app at any time.

2. What we collect and why

Data Purpose Stored where
Email and display name (from Sign in with Apple or Google) Identify your account; show your name in the profile screen. Firebase Authentication (Google Cloud)
User account ID (UID) Tie your listings, photos, and usage counter to your account. Firebase Authentication; referenced locally and in Firestore
Photos you take or pick from your library Sent to Google's Gemini model (via Firebase AI Logic) to identify the item and draft a listing. Stored locally so you can edit and share the listing later. On your device only. Photos are sent transiently to Google for analysis and are not retained by Google for training. See Section 4.
Generated listing details (title, description, price, condition, etc.) Saved as your "garage" of drafted listings. On your device only (SwiftData)
Daily usage counter (dailyUsage/<uid>: date + count) Enforces a daily limit (currently 3 listings/day) to keep AI costs manageable. Firebase Firestore (Google Cloud)
App Attest device attestation token Verifies requests originate from the real Snap n' Sell app on a real Apple device (anti-abuse). Generated per-request by your device. We never see device identifiers from this.

We do not collect: contacts, location, browsing history, your other photos, IDFA, or any analytics about how you use the app beyond what's listed above.

3. Permissions the app requests

  • Camera Required to take photos of items you want to sell. Used only when you tap "Take Photo".
  • Photo Library (Add Only) Optional. Lets the app save a listing photo back to your Photos when you tap "Save Photo to Library".
  • Photo Library (Read) Used only when you tap "Choose Photo" to pick an existing image. We use Apple's modern photo picker, which means we never get access to your full library — only the specific photo you select.

4. Third-party services we use

Snap n' Sell relies on the following services to function. Each link below is to that service's own privacy policy.

  • Firebase Authentication — manages your sign-in. Firebase Privacy
  • Sign in with Apple — when you choose this sign-in option. Apple Privacy
  • Google Sign-In — when you choose this sign-in option. Google Privacy
  • Firebase AI Logic + Google Gemini (via Vertex AI) — generates the listing from your photo. Photos are transmitted to Google for inference. According to Google's Vertex AI data governance, prompt content is not used to train Google models and is not retained for ongoing access.
  • Firebase Firestore — stores only your daily usage counter document keyed by your account ID.
  • Firebase App Check (App Attest) — verifies your app installation is genuine.

5. Data retention and deletion

What stays on your device

Your listings and their photos live only on your iPhone. Sign out doesn't delete them. They sync across your devices only if you've enabled iCloud backup at the system level.

What stays in the cloud

Your authentication record (email, display name, UID) and your dailyUsage/<uid> document persist in Google Cloud until you delete your account.

Deleting your account

Open the app, go to the Me tab, and tap Delete Account. This will:

  • Delete your Firebase Authentication record.
  • Delete your dailyUsage/<uid> document from Firestore.
  • Delete every item in your local "garage" and every photo file the app stored.

This action is immediate and irreversible. We cannot recover deleted data.

6. Children

Snap n' Sell is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect data from children under 13. If you believe a child has signed in, contact us and we'll remove the account.

7. International users

The app's backend infrastructure is hosted by Google Cloud, primarily in the United States. By using Snap n' Sell, you understand that your data may be processed in the U.S. and other countries where Google operates.

8. Your rights

Regardless of where you live, you can at any time:

  • Sign out (Me tab → Sign Out) to stop the app from communicating with your account.
  • Delete your account and all associated data (Me tab → Delete Account).
  • Revoke camera or photo library access from iOS Settings.
  • Email us at huntaj@cofc.edu to request a copy of the data tied to your account.

9. Security

All network traffic between the app and our backend services is encrypted in transit using HTTPS/TLS. Access to your Firestore usage document is restricted by Firebase security rules to your own authenticated account.

That said, no system is perfectly secure. Use strong account credentials and enable two-factor authentication on your Apple or Google account.

10. Changes to this policy

If we materially change how the app handles data, we'll update this page and bump the "Last updated" date at the top. Continued use of the app after a change means you accept the updated policy.
